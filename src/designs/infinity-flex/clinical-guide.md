# Infinity Flex: Clinical Guide & User Instructions

## Intended Purpose

The **Infinity Flex** is a custom-fabricated finger orthosis designed primarily for patients requiring joint stabilization and support due to hypermobility or instability. It can also be useful for managing stability issues associated with Osteoarthritis (OA) and Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA).

This design features a slit option, specifically developed to accommodate patients with significantly enlarged joints where the PIP circumference exceeds the phalanx measurements, allowing the splint to expand during donning and then secure properly for support.

**Note:** The slit may reduce the strength in the orthosis to prevent hyperextension.

## Measurement Instructions

Precise measurements are critical for the parametric design process to ensure a functional fit. For the Infinity Flex, the following data points are necessary:

| Measurement Parameter      | Clinical Guidance                                                                     |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Proximal Phalanx (P1)**  | Measure circumference tightly at the midpoint of the proximal phalanx.                |
| **PIP Joint**              | Measure circumference loosely at the proximal interphalangeal (PIP) joint.            |
| **Middle Phalanx (P2)**    | Measure circumference tightly at the midpoint of the middle phalanx.                  |
| **IP Length**              | Distance from the midpoint of the proximal phalanx to the midpoint of the middle phalanx. |
| **Flexion Angle**          | Input the desired degree of flexion (particularly helpful for hypermobility).         |

**Pro Tip:** Clinicians have found that measuring tightly along the shafts (P1 and P2) while remaining loose at the PIP joint yields the most effective results.

## Instructions for Use

### Fitting & Workflow

1. **Select Design:** Choose the Infinity Flex model from the Splint Factory dashboard.
2. **Input Data:** Enter the precise measurements and select the "slit" feature if required for fitting over a large joint.
3. **Process:** Generate the custom 3D design and release the print job to the SplintBot.
4. **Print:** Select the job from the print queue. Ensure first-layer adhesion for a successful print.
5. **Fitting:** Remove any "skirt" or brim material used for adhesion before placing on the patient.

### The Orientation Dot System

To ensure proper donning by the patient, Splint Factory utilizes an embossed dot system on each orthosis:

- **Purpose:** The dot indicates the proper orientation of the splint (in vs. out) and exactly where it should sit on the finger.
- **If the Dot Protrudes:** It is meant to sit dorsally on the finger.
- **If the Dot is Inset:** It is meant to sit palmarly on the finger.
- **Note:** The dot always sits toward the distal end of the splint.
