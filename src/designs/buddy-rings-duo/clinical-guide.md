# Buddy Ring Duo: Clinical Guide & User Instructions

## Intended Purpose

The **Buddy Ring Duo** is a custom-fabricated finger orthosis designed for patients requiring lateral stabilization or active-assisted range of motion. It can be particularly effective as a step-down intervention for metacarpal fracture management.

Beyond stabilization, this design can be utilized for simple yoke orthosis applications such as intrinsic sprains, or as an exercise orthosis to gain IP flexion or PIP extension. The custom-designed bands provide precise immobilization and protection billable under standard HCPCS code L3933.

**Note:** Adding a slit to the design may accommodate joint swelling or osteoarthritic changes.

## Measurement Instructions

Precise measurements of both individual fingers and their collective perimeter are required to generate a custom 3D design. For the Buddy Ring Duo, the following parameters are necessary:

| Measurement Parameter          | Clinical Guidance                                                                     |
| ------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Left Finger Circumference**  | Measure the circumference of the leftmost finger in the pair, regardless of the hand used. |
| **Right Finger Circumference** | Measure the circumference of the rightmost finger in the pair.                        |
| **Full Perimeter**             | Measure the total circumference around both fingers while they are positioned together. |
| **Forward Offset**             | Specify the distance the right band shifts towards the fingertips (positive) or proximally (negative). |
| **Longitudinal Band Width**    | Specify the desired width in millimeters to set the width of the band as it rests against the fingers. |

**Pro Tip:** To easily measure the offset, measure mid-PIP to mid-PIP joint.

## Instructions for Use

### Fitting & Workflow

1. **Select Design:** Choose "Buddy Ring Duo" from the catalog of designs on the Splint Factory dashboard.
2. **Input Data:** Enter the circumferences and the forward offset value based on patient anatomy.
3. **Process:** Submit the data to generate the custom 3D design and release the print job to the SplintBot (tablet located next to the printer).
4. **Print:** Select the job from the print queue. Ensure first-layer adhesion for a successful print.
5. **Fitting:** Remove any brim material and fit the orthosis directly to the patient during the visit.

### The Orientation Dot System

To assist with proper donning and orientation, the Buddy Ring Duo utilizes the Splint Factory embossed dot system:

- **Purpose:** The dot helps the patient quickly identify the "inside" vs. "outside" orientation of the splint.
- **If the Dot Protrudes:** The splint is oriented to sit dorsally on the finger.
- **If the Dot is Inset:** The splint is oriented to sit palmarly on the finger.
- **Note:** The dot always sits toward the distal end of the splint.
