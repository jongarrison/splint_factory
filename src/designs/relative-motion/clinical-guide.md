# Relative Motion Orthosis: Clinical Guide & User Instructions

## Intended Purpose

The **Relative Motion Orthosis** (RMO), is a custom-fabricated orthosis that positions a finger in relative flexion or extension compared to adjacent fingers. This design promotes the natural cascade of finger flexion and extension, which helps mitigate the secondary effects of immobilization.

Clinical indications include sagittal band injuries, extensor tendon repairs (zones IV-VII), and the treatment of IP joint stiffness. The parametric design process allows for the setting of specific relative angles between fingers to meet clinical goals.

## Measurement Instructions

Precise measurements are critical for the parametric design process to ensure a functional fit. For the Relative Motion Orthosis, the following data points are necessary:

| Measurement Parameter          | Clinical Guidance                                                                     |
| ------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Included Fingers**           | Identify which fingers (Index, Middle, Ring, or Small) are to be included in the splint. |
| **Anchor Finger**              | Designate the specific finger(s) that will act as the stable anchor for the orthosis. |
| **P1 Mid Circumference**       | Measure the circumference tightly at the midpoint of the proximal phalanx for each finger. |
| **P1 Length**                  | Measure the length of the proximal phalanx from the MCP to the PIP.                   |
| **Relative Elevation Angle**   | Input the desired degree of relative elevation or flexion between the fingers.        |
| **Longitudinal Band Width**    | Specify the desired width in millimeters to set the width of the band as it rests against the fingers. |

**Pro Tip 1:** The P1 length can be measured dorsally while the patient is in a fist. If this is contraindicated, this can be done using the pencil test described by [Don Lalonde and Lisa Flewelling](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Lisa-Flewelling).

**Pro Tip 2:** To easily measure the offset, measure mid-PIP to mid-PIP joint.

## Instructions for Use

### Fitting & Workflow

1. **Select Design:** Choose the Relative Motion model from the Splint Factory dashboard.
2. **Input Data:** Enter precise measurements, included fingers, and the desired relative motion angle.
3. **Process:** Generate the custom 3D design and release the print job to the SplintBot.
4. **Print:** Select the job from the print queue. Ensure first-layer adhesion for a successful print.
5. **Fitting:** Remove any adhesion material or brim before fitting the splint to the patient.

### The Orientation Dot System

To ensure proper donning by the patient, Splint Factory utilizes an embossed dot system on each orthosis:

- **Purpose:** The dot indicates the proper orientation and sitting position on the fingers.
- **If the Dot Protrudes:** It is meant to sit dorsally on the finger.
- **If the Dot is Inset:** It is meant to sit palmarly on the finger.
- **Note:** The dot always sits toward the distal end of the splint.
