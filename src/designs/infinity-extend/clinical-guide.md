# Infinity Extend: Clinical Guide & User Instructions

## Intended Purpose

The **Infinity Extend** is a custom-fabricated finger orthosis designed to support the management of Trigger Finger and Boutonniere deformity. By providing targeted extension support, the device helps maintain proper joint alignment and allows for healing and function.

## Measurement Instructions

Precise measurements are critical for the parametric design process to ensure an effective, comfortable fit. For the Infinity Extend, the following data points are typically required for input into the Splint Factory dashboard:

| Measurement Parameter      | Clinical Guidance                                                                     |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Proximal Phalanx (P1)**  | Measure circumference tightly at the midpoint of the proximal phalanx.                |
| **PIP Joint**              | Measure circumference loosely at the proximal interphalangeal (PIP) joint.            |
| **Middle Phalanx (P2)**    | Measure circumference tightly at the midpoint of the middle phalanx.                  |
| **IP Length**              | Distance from the midpoint of the proximal phalanx to the midpoint of the middle phalanx. |
| **Flexion Angle**          | Input the desired degree of flexion (particularly helpful for hypermobility).         |

## Instructions for Use

### Fitting & Workflow

1. **Select Design:** Choose the **Infinity Extend** model from the Splint Factory dashboard.
2. **Input Data:** Enter the precise anatomical measurements taken from the patient into the parametric design fields.
3. **Process:** Generate the custom 3D design and release the print job to the SplintBot.
4. **Print:** Select the job from the print queue. Ensure first-layer adhesion for a successful print.
5. **Fitting:** Remove any "skirt" or brim material used for adhesion. Verify the fit on the patient, ensuring the splint supports the digit without restricting blood flow or compromising comfort.

### The Orientation Dot System

To ensure correct orientation and placement by the patient, Splint Factory utilizes an embossed dot system on each orthosis:

- **Purpose:** The dot indicates the orientation of the splint and its correct anatomical position on the finger.
- **If the Dot Protrudes:** The splint is meant to sit **dorsally** (on the back of the finger).
- **If the Dot is Inset:** The splint is meant to sit **palmarly** (on the palm side of the finger).
- **Placement Note:** The dot is always positioned toward the **distal end** of the splint to ensure correct proximal-distal alignment.
